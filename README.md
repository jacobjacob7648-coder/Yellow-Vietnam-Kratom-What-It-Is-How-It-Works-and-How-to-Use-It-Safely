## How Is Yellow Vietnam Kratom Made?

The yellow color comes from a modified drying process, not from the leaf itself. Fresh kratom leaves are green, white, or red based on vein color. After harvest, producers apply one of several techniques to create the yellow variety.

**Typical production methods include:**

1. **Extended sun drying:** Leaves are dried outdoors for longer than usual, sometimes 2–3 days in direct sunlight. UV exposure changes the alkaloid composition.
2. **Two-stage drying:** Leaves are first dried indoors, then moved to sunlight. This combination shifts the chemical profile toward a milder balance.
3. **Fermentation:** Some producers lightly ferment the leaves before drying. This is less common for yellow strains but does occur.
4. **Blending:** A few vendors mix green and white leaf powder, then label it yellow. This isn't traditional, but it happens.

The lack of standardization is the biggest issue. No regulatory body defines what "yellow" means for kratom. Skip risky claims from vendors who promise specific effects without lab data to back them up. A certificate of analysis (COA) from an independent lab is the minimum standard.

**Best practices for dosing:**

- Avoid mixing with other substances. Consult a doctor if taking medications.
- Rotate strains to reduce tolerance buildup. Don't use the same strain daily.

**Common mistake:** Increasing the dose too quickly when effects feel mild. Yellow Vietnam is a subtle strain. Give it time. Jumping to 5+ grams on a first try is how people end up with nausea and a bad experience.
